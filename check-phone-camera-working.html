How to Check If Your Phone Camera Is Actually Working (No Apps Needed)

A blurry preview, a black screen, or a flash that won't fire — before you assume the worst, run a quick browser test to find out whether it's actually broken or just a stuck setting.

The camera is the part of a phone that gets used the most and explained the least. When something's off — a soft focus, a flash that won't fire, a preview that stays black — most people either live with it or assume the whole camera is dead. Neither is usually the right move. Here's how to actually find out, in under two minutes, without installing anything.

Signs your camera might not be working properly

A few symptoms come up again and again, and each one points toward a different cause:

  • Black or frozen preview — usually a permissions or software issue, not a broken sensor
  • Soft or blurry focus that never sharpens — can be a stuck autofocus motor or, less often, a scratched lens
  • Odd color tint (purple, green, pink) — often points to sensor damage, especially after a drop
  • Flash doesn't fire — check the flash toggle first, since this is more often a setting than a hardware fault
  • Front camera works, rear doesn't (or vice versa) — narrows the problem to one specific module

Test your camera in the browser, not just your camera app

Your phone's built-in camera app applies its own processing — stabilization, auto-exposure, sometimes AI sharpening — which can mask a hardware issue or, just as often, make a software glitch look like a hardware one. Testing straight from the browser gives you a more direct read on what the camera hardware is actually capturing.

What to look for during the test

Once the preview loads, don't just glance at it — actually check these four things:

  • Preview loads within a second or two, without freezing
  • Image sharpens within half a second of pointing at something with detail (like text)
  • Colors look accurate against a known object, no strong tint
  • Switching between front and rear cameras works both ways

When it's software, and when it's hardware

The fastest way to tell them apart: if the camera behaves the same way in your browser as it does in your regular camera app, the issue is very likely hardware. If it works fine in one but not the other, it's almost always software — a stuck permission, a corrupted cache, or a bug introduced by a recent update. Restarting the phone fixes a surprising number of "software" camera issues before you need to dig any further.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my phone camera showing a black screen?
A black screen almost always means an app doesn't have camera permission, or another app is already using the camera in the background. Close other camera apps and check your browser's site permissions before assuming it's a hardware fault.

Can a software update break my camera?
Yes. A buggy OS or app update can cause focus issues, crashes, or a black preview without any hardware damage at all. Testing in a browser, outside of your regular camera app, helps you tell the two apart.

How do I know if my camera problem is hardware or software?
If the camera fails the same way in your browser as it does in your regular camera app, it points to hardware. If it works fine in one but not the other, the problem is almost always software or a permissions setting.

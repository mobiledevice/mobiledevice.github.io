Battery Health Check: Is Your Phone Battery Dying Faster Than Expected?

If a full charge doesn't last like it used to, that's not just in your head. Here's how to actually check your battery's health, what a "good" number looks like, and how to tell wear from a genuine problem.

📅 Published Jul 15, 2026 ⏱️ 6 min read 🏷️ Battery

Every lithium-ion battery loses a little capacity every time it's charged — that's not a defect, it's just chemistry. The question worth answering isn't "is my battery degrading," because it always is; it's "how much, and is it further along than it should be for the phone's age."

Signs your battery health is worse than it should be

  • A full charge lasts noticeably less than half a day of normal use
  • The percentage drops in sudden jumps rather than smoothly (say, 40% to 25% in a few minutes)
  • The phone shuts down unexpectedly while still showing 15–20% remaining
  • The phone gets unusually warm during normal charging, not just fast charging
  • Charging seems to slow down dramatically after 80%, more than it used to

For the most exact figure, check your phone's own settings alongside the browser estimate — iPhone shows a precise peak capacity percentage under Settings > Battery > Battery Health, while Android's reporting varies by manufacturer (see our iPhone vs Android testing comparison for more on that difference).

What speeds up battery wear

A few habits accelerate normal battery aging faster than typical use:

  • Regularly letting the battery drop to 0% before charging
  • Leaving the phone on charge in direct sunlight or a hot car
  • Using the phone heavily while it's plugged in and charging
  • Using a cheap, uncertified fast charger not rated for the phone

When to repair, replace the battery, or replace the phone

If the phone is otherwise in good shape and everything else tests fine, a battery replacement is almost always cheaper than a new phone and brings it right back to feeling new. If you're testing a phone before buying it secondhand, weak battery health isn't necessarily a dealbreaker — just factor the cost of a replacement into your offer. Frequently asked questions

What percentage of battery health is considered bad?
Below 80% capacity is when most people start noticing a real difference in how long the phone lasts. Below 70% is generally when a replacement is worth considering.

Does a browser battery test give the same number as my phone's settings?
It's an estimate that should track closely with your phone's own reporting, but for the most precise figure — especially on iPhone, which shows an exact peak capacity percentage — check your phone's built-in battery settings directly.

Can a battery health issue be fixed without replacing the battery?
Software can sometimes reduce the symptoms — closing battery-hungry apps, disabling background refresh, lowering screen brightness — but it can't restore lost capacity. Once a battery has degraded, replacement is the only fix for the underlying cause.

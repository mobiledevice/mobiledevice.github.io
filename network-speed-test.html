Frequently asked questions about speed testing

A network speed test measures your internet connection's download speed, upload speed, and ping (latency). It helps you understand your Wi-Fi or mobile data performance.

Open this page and tap "Start Speed Test". The tool will measure your download speed, upload speed, and ping using a simulated file transfer.

Yes. The tool works on iPhone, iPad, and iPod touch through Safari. It measures speed using browser-based network requests.

Yes. The network speed test is completely free, with no signup, no ads, and no data collected.

Look for download speed (how fast you receive data), upload speed (how fast you send data), and ping (connection latency). Higher download/upload speeds and lower ping are better.

For basic browsing and email: 5-10 Mbps. For streaming HD video: 15-25 Mbps. For 4K streaming and gaming: 50+ Mbps. For multiple users/device: 100+ Mbps.

Ping (also called latency) measures the time it takes for data to travel from your device to a server and back. Lower ping (<50ms) is good for gaming and video calls. Higher ping (>150ms) can cause lag.

Network speeds can vary due to network congestion, distance from router, number of connected devices, and time of day. Run multiple tests at different times for a more accurate picture.
